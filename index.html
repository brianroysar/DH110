Encouraging the use of Jakarta’s Public Transportation

Brian Roysar | DH 110 | Fall 2022

This project will be centered on the 11th Sustainable Development Goal (SDG), Sustainable Cities and Communities, established by the United Nations (UN). In particular, this project will be focusing on encouraging the use of Jakarta’s public transportation system that includes the newly built MRT, Transjakarta Bus, Train and more. Through this project, I want to create an app that users use to find routes and fun things to do through the use of public transportation.

Introduction

Being the 17th most polluted country in the world, in addition to Jakarta’s average Air Quality Index (AQI) of 110, there should be a larger emphasis on reducing the city’s reliance on private modes of transport, and instead improving the sustainability of many of our public transportation methods to maintain a good quality of life and society in the long term. In addition, having lived in Jakarta my entire life, I've experienced first-hand how inefficient transportation and commuting can be. A lack of structured roads and public transportation only being in its early stages of development, the lack of accessible and efficient transportation has frustrated many Indonesians as commute times and congestion are exponentially high.

Design Statement

Due to the mix of old, legacy transportation systems, such as the train, combined with pre-mature new systems such as the MRT, there is a lack of cohesion when it comes to finding information about routes that utilize multiple systems at once. This means that users will often have to cross reference many different websites and resources to plan out their route that they want to travel, or resort to Google Maps which does not have real time updates on information such as arrival/departure times, delays and cost of route. This acts as a barrier for people to oftenly use the public transportation system as they find it difficult and troublesome to find their way around. Thus, I want to create an application that integrates all the information from all the different public transportation systems, and allow users to find their desired routes, in addition to fun/recommended things to do along the way.

Competitor Analysis

Before we start on ideating and designing the application we want to build, I conducted an analysis on existing solutions for the same problem. This involved performing a heuristic analysis on two similar websites https://jakartamrt.co.id/en and https://transjakarta.co.id/ , where I applied the 10 Usability Heuristics of User Interface Design and provided Severity Ratings for each heuristic. From this, I recognized how difficult it is to navigate through the websites, non-uniform and bland design, and the large amounts of unnecessary clutter that the website has.

After this, I conducted Usability Testing on https://jakartamrt.co.id/en , where I had someone interact with the website, survey their initial thoughts, and complete a set of tasks. From this activity, I was able to gauge the effectiveness, efficiency and satisfaction of the website’s user experience from the perspective of a potential user that might not be initially familiar with the website. From the testing, I was able to learn more insights on what I should improve on in my application and also the positives that I should keep in my application’s user experience.

User Research

To learn more about a user’s wants and needs, I conducted user research that was detached from any existing applications. Through this user research, I gave the interviewee freedom to complete the given tasks in the way they normally would. This involved a mix of interview and participatory observations, where I would ask them questions on how they go about using public transportation and observe how they do it. From this exercise, I was able to learn more about the user’s priorities and preferences. For example, I learn that they prioritize the necessary, simple information about the route, while also their desire to learn more about things to do on the side.

UX Storytelling/User Persona

To understand the potential users that will be using the application, I did UX storytelling by creating personas and scenarios. UX storytelling is essential for us to detach our own thoughts, wants, emotions from the problem we are trying to tackle. It forces us to reshift our perspective to a plethora of different users perspectives, and from that exercise, we are able to have a more holistic understanding of the problem and how we will solve it. It ultimately allows us to be in the shoes of potential users in the real world and make the product focus more on the user's needs and goals. In this exercise, I created two detailed and different personas, while also creating scenarios each one would be in, and their respective journey maps when completing the tasks/scenarios.

Low Fidelity Prototype

At this point, I have decided on the two features that I want to prototype which are:

  1. Integrated Route-Finding: Allows users to find routes that utilize multiple transit systems (Bus, MRT, Train) all in one application. User will also have multiple inputs that they can use to tailor their search (source/destination, time, system preferences, areas to avoid (safety), intermediate stops along the way)
  2. Recommended Activities: Presents users with fun things to do, restaurants/cafes, tourist attractions and more that are located along their route and/or nearby their final destination.

Using Figma, I created a low-fidelity prototype that roughly sketches the barebone structure and logical flow of the application for the two features aforementioned. Through this quick prototyping method, I was able to assess what is feasible and not, while creating a good foundation to build future iterations on

Using the Lo-Fi Prototype, I also conducted testing to see if a user would find the structure and flow easy to understand, learn and use. Through this, I was able to verify the elements that worked smoothly, but also obtain observations on certain flows and elements that seemed unclear to a new user.

Interface Design

Before we transition into the high fidelity prototype, I have to decide on the different design decisions such as layout, shape variations, typography and color. In this phase, I applied different designs on the home screen of the application, and chose the best one that fit the application. In addition, I created both a light and dark mode, while also adhering to the color contrast ratio standard. Here are the final design decisions I decided to use:

  1. 12 x 10 grid for screen
  2. 6 x 6 grid for component
  3. Euclid Circular B font, body spacing of 1.5
  4. 10px roundness
  5. Grey/Blue color palette

Cognitive Walkthrough

After creating the high-fidelity prototype, I was able to perform a cognitive walkthrough on the application. Through this I was able to understand how a user would interact with the app while obtaining first impressions from potential users. This was also a quick and efficient way to obtain feedback and criticism on how I could improve and fix the application's user experience. The key takeaways from the cognitive walkthrough were:

  • User Interface is clean, simple and easy to use
  • Navigation through the application is easy to follow
  • Requires more emphasis on carbon emissions saved. This could be achieved through a an overall daily, weekly and monthly total of how much a user saves on emissions.
  • List of hobbies to choose from is too long and overwhelming. This could be solved through implementing a search/filter feature.

Conclusion

This project was my first time conducting and applying a human-centered design process and it showed me the importance of having the user in mind throughout the entire journey. Having a clear goal in mind, while also having multiple insights from potential users, it made the designing process much smoother and directed as designing felt more objective rather than open-ended.

Throughout the process, I have gained many valuable skills that I will continue to use in the future. Activities such as usability testing and user research allowed me to develop interviewing skills as well as preparing interview material such as the script and Google Form. Creating the low-fidelity prototype, interface design and high-fidelity prototype gave me the opportunity to improve my Figma skills, while familiarizing myself with the many useful plugins that Figma provides such as Arc, Stark and Autoflow.

The most challenging part of the process was the interface design, as I found it difficult to choose a specific design that I felt content with. It required a lot of iteration, and testing out different combinations of shape, color, typography. However, after thinking about which of these factors would be most appropriate for the application, it became a lot easier to decide.

Moving forward, I would love to have the chance to explore more functionalities that Teleport could incorporate, such as a social or safety feature, and expand the prototype as well. I would also want to perform Usability Testing on my prototype to gain even further insights on what can be improved on. Finally, I would also spend some more time creating designs for the potential notifications that a user would receive when they have this application installed, such as route information, safety alerts, and recommended activities.
